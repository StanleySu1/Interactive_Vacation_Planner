U.S. News Insider Tip: Shuttle passengers should pack a bag for the day in Zion Canyon with high-energy snacks (if hiking), a water bottle, a hat and sunscreen along with a layer, just in case. – Catherine Parker

The 3-mile round-trip Watchman Trail doesn't ascend the Watchman Spire, but it does afford a spectacular view of the Watchman, Temples and Towers, lower Zion Canyon and the town of Springdale. As the trail only ascends about 400 feet, it's a good path to start out on for those new to hiking and features desert cacti and greenery along the way.

Previous travelers called this a "nice moderate hike," though some recommend doing it early in the day to avoid the direct sunlight that bears down at midday. Since the trailhead is near the Zion Canyon Visitor Center, reviewers recommend skipping the long lines for the free shuttle in favor of hopping on this trail first. Travelers report spending about two hours hiking the trail.

Hikers will find the trailhead for the Watchman Trail near the main Zion National Park Visitor Center, across from its shuttle stop; the visitor center offers a restroom and drinking water. Hikers should remember to bring plenty of water, slather on sunscreen and start the hike as early as possible, since this trail is fully exposed to the blazing Utah sun. Park admission is required. To learn more about the Watchman Trail, check out Zion National Park's website.
