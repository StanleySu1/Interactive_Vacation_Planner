U.S. News Insider Tip: Zion Lodge offers a large grassy area in front that is a popular place for picnics and post-hike relaxation. Beer, coffee drinks and light snacks are available to purchase seasonally on the patio. – Catherine Parker

Zion Canyon Scenic Drive is one of four main roads in Zion National Park. Along this road, visitors can access famous park landmarks, including Zion Lodge, Angel's Landing, the Court of the Patriarchs and Weeping Rock, to name a few. For much of the year, the only way to access Zion Canyon Scenic Drive is via the park's free shuttle bus. Since 2000, the shuttle bus has carried visitors up and down Zion Canyon, reducing traffic issues along with improving the canyon experience. The shuttle operates daily from March through November. In winter (usually weekdays in December and daily in January and February), visitors can drive their own vehicle along Zion Canyon Scenic Drive.

How long is the Zion Canyon Scenic Drive?

Zion Canyon Scenic Drive is approximately 7 miles long. The up-canyon shuttle routes takes about 45 minutes to reach the last stop, Temple of Sinawava. The entire round-trip ride takes about an hour and a half.

Recent travelers praised the shuttle bus for its frequency (it runs every 5 to 10 minutes). Several mentioned it took the headache out of parking, especially in the narrow sections of Zion Canyon. All said the views from the shuttle route were stunning. Wintertime travelers enjoyed the less busy Zion Canyon and the ability to drive up Zion Canyon Scenic Road in their car.

Rides on the shuttle bus are included in Zion National Park's $35 entrance fee, which grants visitors access to the park for seven days. Drivers can park in the main parking lot at the visitor center and catch the shuttle bus at its first stop. When that parking lot fills, the town of Springdale offers paid parking in which visitors can use the seasonal Springdale shuttle to reach Zion's entrance and the canyon shuttle bus.

Keep in mind that amenities are few and far between along Zion Canyon Scenic Drive. Zion Lodge offers a year-round restaurant and seasonal snack bar. There are no other places where food is sold inside the park. Zion Canyon Visitor Center and the Zion Human History Museum (the only visitor center located along the scenic drive) sells reusable water bottles and visitors will find hydration stations at the Zion Human History Museum (shuttle stop No. 2), Zion Lodge (shuttle stop No. 5), The Grotto (shuttle stop No. 6) and Temple of Sinawava (shuttle stop No. 9). As for restrooms and vault toilets, they are located at the Zion Canyon Visitor Center, the Zion Human History Museum, Zion Lodge, The Grotto, Weeping Rock, the Temple of Sinawava and the South and Watchman Campgrounds.

For hikers using the shuttle to reach trailheads, the canyon shuttle stops at the Pa'rus Trail (Canyon Junction, stop No. 3), Emerald Pools Trail and Angels Landing (The Grotto, stop No. 6), Observation Point Trail (Weeping Rock, stop No. 7) and the Riverside Walk and the start of The Narrows (Temple of Sinawava, stop No. 9). Note for afternoon hikers, the shuttle's last run is shortly after sunset from spring to fall. For more information, visit the Zion National Park shuttle webpage.
