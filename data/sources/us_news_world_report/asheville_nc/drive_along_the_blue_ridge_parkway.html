U.S. News Insider Tip: Stretches of the Blue Ridge Parkway close to vehicles in the winter, which affords you the unique opportunity to experience it by foot. If it’s not too far, park and walk to your hiking destination. – Lindsey Grossman

The 469-mile Blue Ridge Parkway begins in Virginia and meanders through about 250 miles of western North Carolina. With a maximum speed of 45 mph, the scenic byway offers drivers numerous opportunities to stop at overlooks, quaint mountain towns, and hiking and biking trails. The parkway is divided by milepost markers that increase as you drive farther south.

The headquarters and main visitor center is located near Asheville at milepost 384. There, travelers can learn about the natural and cultural history of the region, as well as outdoor activities. Even if you're not up for a road trip along the entire parkway, there are several attractions and trails within 20 miles of Asheville worth exploring, including Craggy Gardens and Mount Mitchell State Park. The Blue Ridge Parkway Association created a handy smartphone app to help customize your adventure with a mile by mile guide and map filled with trails, gas availability, overlooks and more. Download the free app here.

Recent visitors who traveled along the parkway remarked on the abundance of beautiful overlooks, calling it a must-do when visiting Asheville. Many travelers recommended picking up picnic supplies before you hit the road.

Driving along the parkway is free, as is entrance to most of its attractions. Keep in mind: Some attractions, including NPS visitors centers, may be closed due to inclement weather, especially in the colder months. Stretches of the roadway itself are also occasionally closed due to hazardous conditions or maintenance, so be sure to check the website for any notices before you set off. For more information on the parkway, including current closures, visit the National Park Service website.
