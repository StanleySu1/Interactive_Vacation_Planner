How we rank things to do

The skies above Arches National Park are some of the darkest in the continental U.S., and the area was even designated an International Dark Sky Park in 2019 by the International Dark-Sky Association. This means the stargazing opportunities are spectacular. If you stay at the park after the sun goes down, you'll be amazed by crystal clear views of the Milky Way and surrounding constellations. Some of the best places to stargaze include the Windows Section and the Garden of Eden viewpoint. The Devils Garden area near Landscape Arch is also highly recommended. If you choose to hike at night, make sure to stay on the marked trails and pack plenty of supplies.

For the best stargazing conditions, choose a clear night preferably during a new moon, so the light of the moon won't impact the views. The light pollution from downtown Moab can also affect your vision, so it's best to drive as far as you can into the park for the ultimate viewing opportunities. If you want to learn more about astronomy, consider signing up for a stargazing program with a ranger; you can find information about these ranger-led programs at the visitor center. The park is open 24-hours a day, so you can enter any time of night to check out the skies.
