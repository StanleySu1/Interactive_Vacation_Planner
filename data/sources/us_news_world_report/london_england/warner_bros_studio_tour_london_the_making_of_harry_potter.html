U.S. News Insider Tip: Pick an early tour time and explore slowly. There is so much to see that you may miss small details if you go too quickly. And don't worry about time. Once you're inside, you can stay as long as you like (well, until closing). – Marisa Méndez, Senior Editor

Located at the studios where all eight of the "Harry Potter" films were produced, this is a must for anyone wanting to learn more about the Wizarding World. Visitors can view original film sets – from the Great Hall to Gringotts, Diagon Alley to the Gryffindor Common Room – with hundreds of original props and costumes to browse. Glimpse the model used for Hogwarts Castle, learn about Hagrid's animatronic head, visit Platform 9 ¾ and board the Hogwarts Express; this is a comprehensive, interactive tour for the whole family that's well worth adding to your London bucket list.

Recent visitors praised the tour and many said it exceeded their expectations, offering an excellent glimpse into all things Harry Potter with plenty to see (many spent at least three hours exploring the various exhibits). Several travelers mentioned it can get busy, with long lines for the shuttle bus, and some also noted that extras at the shop, cafe and restaurants can be expensive. Visitors were unanimous in saying it was worth the effort, though.

Warner Bros. Studio Tour London is located in Leavesden. Trains depart from London Euston to Watford Junction station and take 20 minutes; from there shuttle buses run every 30 minutes, and take 15 minutes (this is included with your entry ticket).

Hours

The studio is open daily from 8:30 a.m. to 10 p.m. (the first tour begins at 9 a.m., and the last is at 6:30 p.m.). However, hours may vary by season and special events, like the Dark Arts celebration in fall. 

Tickets

Admission costs start at 53.50 pounds (about $67) for anyone 16 and older and 43 pounds (about $54) for children age 5 to 15. Advance booking is essential. Find out more and book on the website.
