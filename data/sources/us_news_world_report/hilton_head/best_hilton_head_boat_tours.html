U.S. News Insider Tip: Adventure Hilton Head, located near Broad Creek Marina, is an awesome way to enjoy Hilton Head views for the restless heart. Aerial rope courses and zip lining tours give visitors a view of the island from atop the pines like no other. – Carmen DeCecco

You can't visit Hilton Head without getting out on the water. With spectacular wildlife, fascinating history and scenic ocean views, the island has just as much to offer on the water as it does on land. Whether you're interested in a sunset dinner cruise, a fishing charter or an informative tour of Daufuskie Island, Hilton Head has a boat tour for every type of traveler.

Vagabond Cruise, Lowcountry Watersports, Shelter Cove Boat Tours and Outside Hilton Head all received high ratings from recent visitors. These companies offer an array of different cruises and tours, ranging from dolphin cruises, charter fishing trips and guided kayaking tours. Pirates of Hilton Head is another tour loved by families for its fun and whimsical pirate cruise; it departs out of both Harbour Town. This interactive cruise (recommended for ages 2 through 10) takes place on the calm Calibogue Sound and runs for an hour and a half. While aboard, children will enjoy face painting, a water cannon fight and lessons in how to speak like a pirate from the crew of the Black Sparrow.  

Rates vary depending on tour type and length, but travelers can expect to pay at least $40 per person. Some companies may offer lower rates when you book with a group.
