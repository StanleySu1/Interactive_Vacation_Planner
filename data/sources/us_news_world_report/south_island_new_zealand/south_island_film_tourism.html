How we rank things to do

It's been two decades since Peter Jackson's "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy cemented New Zealand's place as a Hollywood filming hot spot, and continuing growth of the screen sector has only boosted film tourism.

More than 150 locations were used to create Middle-earth, and while the Hobbiton Movie Set remains a popular North Island location, the South Island is home to much of the stunning scenery featured in "The Lord of the Rings" and "The Hobbit" trilogies. In the Nelson Tasman region, helicopter tours provide a bird's-eye view into Dimrill Dale (Mount Olympus and Mount Owen), where the fellowship took cover when Saruman unleashed black crows. By car, you can drive to Takaka Hill, which doubled as Chetwood Forest (where hobbits tried to escape the Black Riders). Marlborough's Pelorus River is where dwarves rode down the river in barrels in "The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug." You can swap the barrel for a kayak to experience the river ride yourself with a tour company like Pelorus Eco Adventures.

The Canterbury region is home to Edoras (the real Mount Sunday), and Pelennor Fields (located in Twizel), where the men of Gondor and Rohan battled orcs. The fields were also used in "The Hobbit" trilogy, as was nearby Lake Pukaki, better known to Middle-earth fans as Laketown.

In Queenstown, Chard Farm vineyard has a view of the Pillars of Kings (though the statues were created with computers), or you can drive to the summit of the Crown Range to peer down on multiple filming locations.

An approximately 30-mile drive northwest of Queenstown lies Glenorchy which provided backdrops for the Middle-earth films. The lakeside forest that served as Lothlórien is on the way. Meanwhile, the Waiau River, between Te Anau and Manapouri, is where the fellowship faced an action-packed journey along the River Anduin. Fictional Fangorn Forest is also near Te Anau, as is the Eglinton Valley (a stop on Altitude Tours' Milford Sound package), where a battle scene was filmed. Dedicated devotees can also book tailored excursions like Pure Glenorchy's Lord of the Rings Scenic Tour and Red Carpet Tours' multiday trips, which have won high praise from movie buffs.

Beyond Tolkien-inspired films, the South Island has provided the backdrop for a variety of other films, including "X-Men Origins: Wolverine" (Hugh Jackman was portrayed jumping off Stirling Falls in Milford Sound). Both "The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, The Witch and The Wardrobe" and "The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian" filmed scenes in Glenorchy. The Otago region doubled for 1920s Montana with a Hawkdun Range farm serving as the Burbank ranch in "The Power of the Dog."

You can learn more about the country's famous filming locations by visiting the Pure New Zealand website.
