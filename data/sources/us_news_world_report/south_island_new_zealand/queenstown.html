U.S. News Insider Tip: If you visit Walter Peak High Country Farm, leave ample time to stroll its stunningly beautiful lakefront garden – the perfect location to snap a new profile photo. – Leena Tailor, Contributor, Travel

The birthplace of commercial bungee jumping, Queenstown/ Tahuna is considered the "adventure capital of the world," but there's so much more to this resort town than thrill-seeking. Nestled against Lake Wakatipu, the city's name has been linked with being fit for Queen Victoria and has hosted royals like Prince William, Kate Middleton and Queen Elizabeth II, as well as celebrities like Reese Witherspoon and Justin Bieber.

Winter is the time for hitting the slopes at Queenstown's ski resorts like Cardrona, Treble Cone, Coronet Peak and The Remarkables, followed by a soothing soak in cedar tubs at Onsen Hot Pools, then a hearty dinner of high country lamb and seafood at lakeside cottage-turned-restaurant Botswana Butchery Queenstown. Remember: Wintertime is June through October.

Summer months (December through March) are all about hanging by the lake eating the town's famed Fergburger (so good that Ed Sheeran once had the Fergburger team head to Dunedin – about 170 miles southwest – to make him "the best burger in the world"), riding the Skyline Queenstown gondola approximately 1,500 feet up to the world's most scenic luge, sipping pinot noir like Prince William and Kate Middleton at Amisfield and meandering through the Queenstown Gardens. Thrill-seekers will love an adrenaline-pumping boat ride on Shotover Jet, owned by Ngai Tahu, the South Island's principal Maori iwi (tribe). Learn more about Maori culture and the legend behind Lake Wakatipu with KJet's Queenstown Underwater Experience.

Other things to do include hopping aboard the century-old, coal-fired steamship TSS Earnslaw for a 90-minute cruise to Walter Peak High Country Farm, where a gourmet barbecue buffet and farm demonstration awaits. Or, head to the historic gold-mining area of Arrowtown, where visitors can walk through the ruins of an 1800s Chinese mining settlement and rent gold-mining pans to try their luck in the river. Back on the town's main strip, learn more about the charming area and its royal ties at Lakes District Museum & Gallery or grab a flat white coffee at one of several quaint cafes.

Queenstown is also about a 45-mile drive south of Lake Wanaka, which buzzes during the summer months with paddle-boarding and pedal-boating (rent equipment from traveler-approved operator Lakeland Wanaka). Popular things to do in Wanaka also include fly-fishing, hiking up Roy's Peak, grabbing food or drinks at lively spots like Federal Diner, snapping a selfie with #thatwanakatree or visiting wineries like Rippon, which is worth a stop for it's incredible lake views alone.

Accommodation options range from hostels to luxurious lakeside rooms and apartments at The Rees Hotel, resorts you won't want to leave like Hilton Queenstown Resort & Spa and splurge-worthy accommodations like the Sofitel Queenstown Hotel and Spa.

Queenstown is approximately a two-hour flight from Auckland, and most visitors agree you'll need a few days to fully explore the area and its surroundings. For more information, visit the Queenstown tourism bureau website or U.S. News' Queenstown travel guide.
