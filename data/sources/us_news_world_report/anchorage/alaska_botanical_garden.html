U.S. News Insider Tip: Guided tours of the garden are a great way to become familiar with Alaska’s native plants, and it’s worth the cost to have a personal guide for your visit. – Erin Kirkland

Nestled in 110 acres of boreal forest, the Alaska Botanical Garden features 150 native plant species and more than 1,100 perennials. There is an herb garden, a wildflower trail, two perennial gardens and the Anchorage Heritage Garden, which was completed in the summer of 2015 and is filled with native plants that were grown from local seeds.

Called a "hidden gem" by recent visitors, the garden attracts both locals and visitors alike with its serene walking paths, beautiful flowers and helpful staff that are available to answer any questions you may have about the gardens. Others appreciated the educational placards stationed throughout the garden.

At the south end of the garden is the Lowenfels-Hoersting Family Nature Trail and burbling Campbell Creek, which has a summer Chinook salmon run. Plus, visiting in the warmer months guarantees that you will see thousands of flowering plants.

The Alaska Botanical Garden is located about 2½ miles southeast of the University of Alaska Anchorage and shares a parking lot with Benny Benson Secondary School. The garden is also accessible via route No. 25 on the People Mover. From June to August, the garden operates a shuttle that provides service between downtown and the garden Wednesday through Sunday from 11:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Garden admission rates vary by season: tickets cost $5 per adult between September and May and $14 per adult between May and September. The garden welcomes visitors between May 15 and Sept. 10 from noon to 7 p.m. Sunday and 10 a.m. to 7 p.m. Monday through Saturday. For more information, visit the garden's website.
