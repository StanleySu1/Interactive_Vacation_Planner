U.S. News Insider Tip: As you’re driving, keep your eyes peeled for colorful food trucks along the side of the road like Cactus Blue, which features unique local fare like lionfish burgers on its lunch menu. – Susan Campbell

No trip to Bonaire is complete without taking a trip along the south coast to experience some of the island’s most surreal signature sights. Locally referred to as the “Rondje Zuid,” meaning the “South Round,” this sightseeing route begins downtown and heads south along the coast past Donkey Beach and Bachelor’s Beach to the famous salt mountains, which are flanked by pink salt pan lakes now run by Cargill. Also of note on this route is the Willemstoren Lighthouse. Built in 1837, it still operates today, though it has been modernized to run on solar power. Beside it sits the ruins of the original lightkeeper’s house; the scene makes for excellent photographs by the sea.

This route also leads to the flamingo sanctuary. Many visitors travel this route on their own by rental car or golf cart since it’s an easy drive. If you venture out on your own, don’t forget your snorkel gear and a towel as the Salt Pier – a popular dive site – is also an excellent spot to see sea turtles and lots of fish. But be forewarned that there is no public access to the pier when a ship is berthed at the dock for loading salt. The full South Round route continues on to Lac Bay, but you are better off to save that region and the mangrove explorations there for another day to fully appreciate all it has to offer.

If you’re not up for renting your own car or golf cart, you can sign up for a guided tour. Almost every island tour will include and stop along this route, but some options afford you a better open-air view along the way, such as Bon Tuk Eco-Tours Bonaire. The company uses electric tuk-tuks that can seat between four and seven people. 

Recent tourgoers appreciated the quiet, eco-friendly tuk-tuks, calling the experience a fun ride with well-informed guides. Reviewers also said the guides helped them understand the significance of several of the historical sights along the route, such as the slave huts at the salt pans.

The other most popular sightseeing drive you can easily do on your own is the “North Round” (Rondje Noord). Similar to the South Round, this route traces the coast and starts downtown. Highlights along the North Round include Rincon and the 1000 Steps dive site, a famous staircase that leads to a rocky cove full of fish, so called because it feels like 1,000 steps when hiking up the steps with your diving tanks (in reality, there are only a total of 67 steps).
