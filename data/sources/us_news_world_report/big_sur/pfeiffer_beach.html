U.S. News Insider Tip: Just south of Pfeiffer Beach sits the Henry Miller Memorial Library. This memorial to former Big Sur resident and writer Henry Miller – one of the most famous banned authors in American history – doubles as a free speech zone and venue for concerts, community events and speakers each year. – Kristy Alpert

If you're on a tight itinerary and don't have time to traverse any state park trails, head to Pfeiffer Beach. The turnoff for this secluded beach is located about 1 mile south of Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park's entrance, where you'll find Pfeiffer Beach's expansive shorelines covered by towering, vegetation-heavy bluffs and striking rock formations. The beach's most famous rock formation, Keyhole Rock, is located a few feet out into the ocean.

Pfeiffer Keyhole Rock

The arch at Pfeiffer Beach, known to locals as "Pfeiffer Keyhole Rock," is hard to miss. It's considered one of the most-photographed attractions in Big Sur. At low tide, visitors are able to wade through the water and take a look at the surrounding tide pools. But the best time to see the rock is at sunset when the sun shines brightly through the keyhole, creating a spectacular photo-op.

Tips for visiting

Although it's secluded, Pfeiffer Beach can get crowded; some recent visitors reported having to wait in line just to secure a parking spot. But despite the inconvenience, travelers said a visit to the beach to see the unique rock formation was worth the hassle. Many strongly suggested bringing a jacket, as the area can get windy and also said that even if you're not a photographer, you should bring a camera or make sure your phone is charged to capture the impressive scenery.

How to get to Pfeiffer Beach

The beach is located off of Highway 1, but there are no entrance signs, and the road to get to Pfeiffer Beach is also unmarked. Sycamore Canyon Road is only distinguishable by its conditions (it's the only paved, ungated road west of Highway 1 between the Big Sur Post Office and Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park). When driving south, keep your eyes out for a yellow Narrow Road sign and make the turn.

Entrance fees

Entrance to Pfeiffer Beach costs $15 per vehicle. There is no camping allowed and due to the geography of the beach, swimming is considered dangerous. Although they share the same name, Pfeiffer Beach is not located in Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park or Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park.

Hours

The beach is open daily from 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.
