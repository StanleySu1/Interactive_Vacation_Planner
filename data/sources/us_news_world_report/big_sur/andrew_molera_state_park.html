This almost 5,000-acre park is the largest state park on the Big Sur coast and offers multiple activities and attractions, not to mention several hiking trails.

Highlights

Named after the man who brought the artichoke to California, Andrew Molera State Park's natural wonders include secluded beaches, redwood groves, meadows and the Big Sur River along its 20 miles of hiking trails. Because of the park's flatter geography, visitors are allowed to bike on select trails. The beach is also safe to surf, and is considered to be one of the best surfing spots in Big Sur. However, there's no lifeguard on duty and the water can be rough, so you'll swim and surf at your own risk.

Andrew Molera State Park Trails

There are 10 trails that comprise Andrew Molera State Park, though some of these are just short spur trails. The Bluffs Trail, Panorama Trail and Ridge Trail are all main trails that connect to make the Andrew Molera Loop. The Loop takes travelers along the coast via the Bluffs Trail then around into an equally beautiful wooded area via the Panorama and Ridge trails. There are also various pathways on the Bluffs Trail that go directly down to the shore. The entire loop is 8 miles.

For those wanting a shorter hike, take the 2-mile Headlands Trail. The trail snakes along the Big Sur River, leading visitors up to the top of Molera point, which affords panoramic views of Andrew Molera beach, the Big Sur River and the Big Sur Valley. Big Sur's oldest building, the Cooper Cabin, is also located along this trail.

Tips for visiting

Recent travelers loved Andrew Molera State Park's scenic coastal trails, with many favoring the beauty of the Headlands Trail and the Bluffs Trail. Others pointed out that although the park is flat in comparison to other areas in Big Sur, there are still elevation changes along the Andrew Molera Loop, and advised travelers to wear sturdy shoes and bring a jacket to combat chilly coastal winds. Note that the park has significant amounts of poison oak. Also, aside from the Headlands Trail and the East Molera Trail, all trails require visitors to cross the Big Sur River. There is a bridge to cross the river, but it is only installed from June to October.

Andrew Molera State Park is less than 25 miles south of Carmel off of Highway 1.

Hours and tickets

The park opens at 8 a.m. and closes at sunset. There is a $10 fee to park your car. For more information about the park's facilities and various offerings, including the Park Discovery Center and Ranch House, visit the Andrew Molera State Park website.
