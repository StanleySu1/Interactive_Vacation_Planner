How we rank things to do

The city of Palm Springs rests in the shadows of the San Jacinto Mountains. The towering, snowcapped peaks of Southern California's second-highest mountain range are beautiful to behold from the Coachella Valley floor, but many visitors say the best views are at the top of the Mt. San Jacinto State Park and Wilderness Area. 

To reach the park, you'll take a 10-minute ride on the Palm Springs Aerial Tramway in tram cars that rotate slowly, offering 360-degree views of Chino Canyon along the way. Once you rise 8,516 feet to the top, you can hike (there are more than 50 miles of trails, including to San Jacinto Peak), or snowshoe and cross-country ski during the winter, before heading back down the mountain. 

If you’re not up for hiking, there are also two restaurants, a cocktail lounge, a snack bar, observation decks, a natural history museum, two documentary theaters and a gift shop at the tramway’s “Mountain Station.”

Tickets

To avoid waiting in long ticket lines, purchase your tram passes in advance on the attraction's ticket page. Only a small portion of tickets are available to book online. If tickets are not available online for your arrival date, they are likely not sold out, you will just need to buy them on-site. Adults will be charged $30.95 for standard tram tickets; tickets for children ages 3 to 10 cost $18.95, and seniors age 65 and older pay $28.95. Parking by the ticket office is also available for $15 per vehicle. Pay by cash or card, just know that bills above $20 are not accepted.

Tips for visiting

Keep in mind: The weather at the top is typically 30 to 40 degrees cooler than the desert floor, so you'll want to wear layers to stay comfortable. This is a boon in summer when the valley becomes extremely hot.

Hours

The Palm Springs Aerial Tramway operates every day. Hours vary by season, but generally, the first tram ascends at 10 a.m. Saturday, Sunday and holidays, and at 8 a.m. Monday through Friday. From Thursday to Sunday, the last tram up is at 8 p.m.; on Friday and Saturday, the last tram up is at 9 p.m.

The base tram station sits about 6 miles west of downtown Palm Springs; it’s best reached by car. Additional information about on-site amenities, opening hours and more can be found on the Palm Springs Aerial Tramway website.
