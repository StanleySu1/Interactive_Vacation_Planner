U.S. News Insider Tip: There are no ties allowed at Pinnacle Peak Steakhouse. If a diner wears a tie, a staff member will cut if off with scissors and hang it from the ceiling. There are thousands of ties hanging, so wear an old one to participate in the tradition! – Jill Schildhouse

On the hunt for a family-friendly activity? Look no further than Trail Dust Town, Tucson's favorite place for "Old West" fun since 1961. A stroll down its main street feels reminiscent of what you might have experienced during the pioneer days – and it's still locally owned and operated by the same family as when it started, three generations ago.

You're in for a treat during your visit, so make sure to allow enough time for all the attractions (rides cost $3 each). The miniature train, carousel and "Circle the Wagons" Ferris wheel operate Thursday to Sunday from 5 p.m. to 9 p.m. Catch the Pistoleros Wild West Show Friday through Sunday at 7 p.m. and 8 p.m. (ticket prices vary). Try your hand at striking it rich with Gabby's Gulch Gold Panning Thursday through Sunday from 5 p.m. to 9 p.m., and test your skills with an interactive rifle in the Shooting Gallery ($0.50 per game). Throughout the property, you'll find photo-ops galore (there's even a portrait studio for some professional shots with the pioneer town backdrop). 

Once you've worked up an appetite, head straight to Pinnacle Peak, Tucson's original cowboy steakhouse. The steaks are cooked over real mesquite wood, but there are vegetarian dishes too (and a children's menu). For an after-dinner drink, pop by The Silver Dollar Saloon, an 1890s-style bar with local draft beers, cocktails and wines. Breakfast at Millie's Pancake Haus is second-to-none, and a local favorite since 1964. Most everything's made from scratch, from pancakes and blintzes to potato pancakes and Belgian waffles.

In the mood for a little shopping? The Trail Dust Trading Company sells souvenirs, such as wooden nickels, while Toys-N-Tiques is the "general store" filled with novelties and nostalgic candy. There's a magic shop and artisan jeweler on-site, too. Finally, follow your nose to the Chocolate Depot, where you'll find decadent handmade Belgian chocolates, fudges and brittle, plus ice cream and cappuccinos.

Past visitors said this was a great activity for young kids, and were especially impressed with the entertaining show. However, others lamented that the attractions are only open in the evening.

Hours vary by activity; most operate Thursday through Sunday evening. There is no entrance fee for Trail Dust Town, but there is a fee per activity. Trail Dust Town sits less than 10 miles northeast of downtown Tucson and is best reached by car. For more information, visit the official website.
