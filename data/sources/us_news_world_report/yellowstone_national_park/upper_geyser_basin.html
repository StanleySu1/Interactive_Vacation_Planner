U.S. News Insider Tip: Consider renting a bike to explore this area. You can rent bikes for an hour or the whole day at the Old Faithful Snow Lodge. Keep in mind, biking isn't permitted on the boardwalks near the geysers. – Catherine Parker

More than its star attraction (Old Faithful), the Upper Geyser Basin boasts not only the largest concentration of geysers in the park, but also in the entire world (Yellowstone is home to 60% of the world's geysers). So, after seeing Old Faithful erupt, be sure to hike around to discover the more than 150 hydrothermal features throughout this area.

It's important to know that Upper Geyser Basin is pretty big, stretching from Biscuit Basin in the north to Old Faithful in the south and Black Sand Basin to the east, with plenty of geysers and hot springs in between. Visitors can certainly see all areas in one day, with Biscuit Basin and Old Faithful located a little more than 3 miles apart and connected by a series of trails, boardwalks and a bike path. If you really enjoyed Old Faithful's eruption and would like to see more, know that Riverside, Daisy, Grand and Castle geysers all erupt and the National Park Service offers estimated eruption times at the visitor center or via the NPS app. If you really liked the Grand Prismatic Spring and want to see more colorful pools, check out the Morning Glory Pool and the Sapphire Pool.

Recent visitors to the Upper Geyser Basin agreed that there is so much more to this area than Old Faithful and urged future travelers to check out its other natural features. Travelers were amazed by the unique geothermal landscape and really appreciated the easy-to-navigate walkways that led to each natural wonder. Some travelers in particular recommended planning exactly where you want to go ahead of time, especially if you have young kids in tow. A couple recommended packing snacks or a picnic lunch to enjoy the outdoors instead of a restaurant.

Upper Geyser Basin is about 32 miles southeast of the west entrance of Yellowstone. The attraction has no set hours and entrance to the basin is included in the park's seven-day admission fee ($35 per vehicle). It's extremely important while in the area to stay on marked pathways and keep kids close (pets are prohibited). The average temperatures of geyser and hot spring pool hovers around 200 degrees Fahrenheit. People who have gotten too close have suffered severe or fatal burns. It's also important to know that sometimes these hydrothermal areas can emit toxic gases. The NPS states that if you suddenly start to feel sick, leave the area and if you are able, alert an NPS staff member. For more information on the Upper Geyser Basin, visit Yellowstone National Park's website.
