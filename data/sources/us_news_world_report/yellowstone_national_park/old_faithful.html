U.S. News Insider Tip: Escape the crowds directly in front of Old Faithful and enjoy the geyser's eruption from the second-floor outdoor balcony of the Old Faithful Inn, another national treasure. Visitors will find a coffee bar just inside. – Catherine Parker

This world-renowned geyser is a must-see for every Yellowstone visitor. Although it isn't the largest active geyser in the world (that would be the Steamboat Geyser in the Norris Geyser Basin), Old Faithful's eruptions are definitely awe-inspiring, reaching heights of 106 to 180 feet, and most importantly, frequent. It is one of only six geysers in the park whose eruptions are predictable (Steamboat Geyser is not one of those). As such, Old Faithful lives up to its name. Since 2019, it has erupted approximately every 90 minutes (be sure to check in with the visitor center's sign to get a more accurate prediction), with the eruption lasting from one to five minutes. During this time a whopping 3,700 to 8,400 gallons of water spew out of the geyser.

There are a couple of ways you can behold Old Faithful's power: You can join the hordes of tourists who gather on the boardwalks around the perimeter or you can hike the 1.6-mile out-and-back Observation Point trail for a bird's-eye view of the Upper Geyser Basin.

Many recent visitors chose to gather around the immediate perimeters of the geyser and were stunned by what they saw. They found the eruption to be amazing and worth the wait. Several visitors reported waiting a long time (some more than an hour) for the geyser to erupt, but said the many surrounding benches helped with the wait. It's important to know that because this is the most popular attraction in the park, crowds are to be expected and parking will be limited from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.

Old Faithful is located about 30 miles south of the park's West Entrance. Surrounding Old Faithful are parking lots, several hotels, eateries, a gas station, a medical clinic, a post office and a visitor center. Keep in mind, the Old Faithful Visitor Center is open year-round with seasonal hours. Other facilities generally open in May and close between September and October, so plan accordingly. Access to Old Faithful is included in the park's seven-day admission pass ($35 per vehicle). Visit the National Park Service's Old Faithful Area page for more information.
