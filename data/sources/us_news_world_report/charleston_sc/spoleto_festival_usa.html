U.S. News Insider Tip: If your interests fall closer to the culinary arts than the performing arts, book a trip for the Charleston Wine & Food in early March. The festival packs dinners, seminars, workshops, brunches, street fairs and interactive experiences (like boat trips to working oyster farms) into five days. Local and international food, wine and cocktail talent host the events.  – Lauren Mowery, Contributor

Founded in 1977 by Pulitzer Prize-winning composer Gian Carlo Menotti, Charleston's annual Spoleto Festival USA showcases music, theater, dance and opera performances. The festival draws its name from the city of Spoleto in Italy where Menotti previously founded an arts festival. This 17-day annual event (which runs from late May through early June) draws tens of thousands of visitors, both domestic and international, who come for the festival's unique blend of world-class performances and Southern charm.

Hundreds of events run at venues across the city, making it a truly immersive experience for art and music lovers. Expect talented musical acts performing classical and contemporary works; theater productions spanning plays to experimental performances; and contemporary dancers moving in sync with world-class choreography. A festival highlight is the opera, which performs works by Mozart, Verdi and Puccini, as well as contemporary composers in Charleston's historic Dock Street Theatre.

The Spoleto Festival consistently receives rave reviews with many praising the quality and diversity of the performances, as well as the charming atmosphere of Charleston as the backdrop for the event. Past attendees praised the caliber of the performances and appreciated the opportunity to discover new and exciting artists.

Tickets

Spoleto Festival USA takes place annually in venues across Charleston. Ticket prices vary widely depending on the performance, venue and seating section: A ticket to a small performance can cost as little as $25, while a major opera or theatre production can run $200 per seat. In general, tickets to the festival's larger and more popular performances tend to be more expensive than those for smaller, less well-known shows. The festival also offers a range of ticket packages and discounts, including group rates and teacher discounts. Consider signing up for the festival's email list well ahead of your visit: Subscribers sometimes get promotions for multishow package deals, which saves a bit of cash.

Tickets sell out quickly, particularly for the most popular performances, so purchase in advance to ensure availability. Visitors can purchase tickets online through the festival's official website or by phone through the festival's box office.
