How we rank things to do

Neyland Stadium, located on the campus of the University of Tennessee and built overlooking the Tennessee River, has been home to the university's wildly popular football team for more than a century. Over the years, the Tennessee Vols (as the team is known, short for "Volunteers") have won a handful of national championships, and several players and head coaches have been honored in the College Hall of Fame. After renovations completed in 2022, the stadium's capacity reached 101,915 – making it the sixth-largest college football stadium in the country.

Neyland Stadium tours

Though attending a game is the undoubtedly the best way to experience the stadium's electric atmosphere, that's not the only way to get a sense for the stadium's size and influence. Hourlong behind-the-scenes tours are available during the week when the crowds are gone and the atmosphere is calmer. Tours typically include the media center, the recruiting center, the locker room complex, the Tennessee team tunnel (where the home team runs onto the field on game days), and other notable areas not usually available to the general public. (Note that there is no access to the field itself during tours.) Participants also get a great view of the iconic 12-by-12-foot orange and white letters spelling “VOLS” installed on either side of the video scoreboard on the south end of the stadium.

The field itself dates from 1919 (while the first game played there was in 1921), and the stadium eventually built around it was named for Gen. Robert R. Neyland in 1962. Neyland was the team's head coach from 1926 through 1952 (with two interruptions for military service). His career record at the university was 173 wins, 31 losses, and 12 ties, and his 1939 team was the last to shut out each of its regular season opponents.

Hours and admission

Tours of the stadium, which sits on the campus of the University of Tennessee adjacent to downtown, are available Monday through Thursday by advance reservation only. They begin at 10 a.m. and end at 11 a.m., and the cost is $20 for adults and $10 for children ages 13 and younger.

More information is available on the stadium's website.
