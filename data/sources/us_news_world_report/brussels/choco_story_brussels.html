How we rank things to do

The first ever chocolate museum in Belgium charts the history of the sweet stuff from the Aztecs through to the rise of chocolate in Europe and the invention of the Belgian praline. Along with the history of chocolate, you'll also learn how chocolate is made, starting with the harvesting of cocoa pods and beans and the various techniques used to create the chocolate products we know and love.

Highlights

Fun and interactive tours include a demonstration where visitors can see how pralines are made, and, naturally, there are tasting sessions so you can actually try the chocolates yourself. For a hands-on experience, you can pay extra to participate in a chocolate-making workshop.

Recent reviews

Perhaps unsurprisingly, most visitors were particularly excited to try the free samples, but the demonstrations by chocolatiers also received praise. According to reviewers, the self-guided audio tours are easy to navigate, and the chocolate-making workshops are a particularly great activity for couples. Be advised that because of the timed entries it can get very crowded at certain points.

Choco-Story Brussels is located right in the heart of the historic center, moments from the Grand-Place and Manneken-Pis. It is easily reached on foot from Brussels-Central Station.

Hours and Tickets

The museum is open every day from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Tickets cost 14 euros (around $15) for adults, 12 euros (around $13) for youths ages 12 to 26 and 7.50 euros (around $8) for kids aged 3 to 11. Children younger than 3 enter for free. The entrance fee includes the use of an audio guide and an artisanal chocolate tasting. Note that it's not permitted to take strollers inside.
