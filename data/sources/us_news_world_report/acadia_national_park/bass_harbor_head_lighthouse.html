Constructed in the mid-1800s, Bass Harbor Head Light Station is considered one of the most picturesque lighthouses in all of Maine. It even appeared on the America the Beautiful 2012 quarter. The U.S. Coast Guard still maintains the beacon, but it transferred ownership to Acadia National Park on July 8, 2020. Visitors may not enter the house, but it's best observed from outside, anyway.

The landmark sits on the southern tip of Mount Desert Island and guards the entrance to Bass Harbor and Blue Hill Bay. Visitors can reach the lighthouse by parking at the free lot off Lighthouse Road in Bass Harbor. A level, short path leads to close-up views of the tower's cliff side and a bell. For the best views, take the path through the woods located at the far end of the parking lot. It leads to a steep wooden stairway and then more gradual stone steps to a rugged path over uneven granite rocks to the shoreline. From here, you get a glimpse of the lighthouse. Scramble through the rocky shoreline's boulders for closer views. Travelers with limited mobility should be cautious of the steep trail and the often slippery stones of the path.

Previous visitors said both sunrises and sunsets are spectacular here. Visitors also laud the platform's location as it's the perfect spot to witness groups of dolphins and seals up close. However, in the peak summer months there is often traffic congestion on the drive to the lighthouse, so plan to arrive early. The parking lot is woefully small and parking is prohibited along the access road and Route 102A. During peak months, you'll likely have to wait for a space. You can try to avoid some of the hassle by taking the Island Explorer, which drops passengers off a half-mile from the lighthouse. For more information, visit the National Park Service website.
