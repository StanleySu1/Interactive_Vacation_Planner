How we rank things to do

Located about an hour's drive north of town, Moraine Lake is a visual highlight of any Banff trip and labeled an unforgettable experience by many recent visitors. Nestled in the Valley of the Ten Peaks, the lake mirrors the snowcapped mountains and is surrounded by backcountry hiking trails.

Hiking at Moraine Lake

The easiest trail is the flat Moraine Lakeshore path, which starts near Moraine Lake Lodge and takes about 45 minutes to complete (round-trip). As you walk along the lakeshore, you'll spot stunning views of Mount Fay and the Fay Glacier. If you've got a bit more time on your hands, consider embarking on the nearly 2-mile Consolation Lakes trail, which takes about two hours to complete. You'll encounter some elevation gains with this one, but you'll be rewarded with views of the high alpine meadows, talus slopes and the Quadra Glacier. For a detailed list of all the available hikes in this area, visit the Parks Canada website.

How to get to Moraine Lake

As of 2023, personal vehicles are no longer permitted on Lake Moraine Road; this restriction is in effect year-round. If you want to experience the lake, you must take a Parks Canada shuttle (reserve online here), Roam public transit (route No. 10), hike or bike in, or book an organized sightseeing excursion, such as those offered by Discover Banff Tours. Commercial vehicles are permitted on the road from June to mid-October. Tickets for the Parks Canada shuttle cost CA$8 (about $6) for adults and are free for kids 17 and younger. Shuttles run daily during the season from 6:30 a.m. to 6 p.m.; the last return shuttle is at 7:30 p.m.

When you're not admiring the bright blue waters from the trail (rock silt that's deposited into the lake from melting glaciers contributes to its vibrant hue), consider hopping in a canoe and experiencing it for yourself. You can rent canoes at the nearby Moraine Lake Lodge (rentals are available from mid-June to mid-September), which is also where you'll find restrooms. Canoe rentals are first-come, first-served, with a maximum of three people per canoe. If you can, try to get to the lake early in the morning so that you can enjoy a little more peace during your hike or paddle on the lake. Canoe rentals cost CA$140 plus tax (or about $105).

Recent visitors said Moraine Lake was not as busy as Lake Louise, and just as breathtaking. Many recommended the Parks Canada shuttle, but advised booking your reservation well in advance.

Note: Moraine Lake Road is closed from mid-October to mid-May due to avalanche risk (mid- to late summer is considered the best time to go). Check the Parks Canada website for more information.
