Covering 89 square miles on the northern edge of Banff National Park, the Columbia Icefield is the largest body of ice in the Canadian Rockies. It's a bit of a drive from Banff Town (about two hours) to the Columbia Icefield Discovery Centre across from the foot of the Athabasca Glacier, but you'll quickly find the views are well worth the trip.

How to get to the Columbia Icefield

Seasonal tour buses are available from Banff Town (such as these offered by Discover Banff Tours and Brewster Sightseeing), but consider driving the Icefields Parkway (Route 93) north from Banff to enjoy the stunning trip, which is considered among the most scenic drives in the world. Along the way, you'll spot turquoise lakes, rushing waterfalls and impressive glaciers.

Whether you come on a tour or drive yourself, once you arrive at the Columbia Icefield Discovery Centre, you'll take a bus to the base of the glacier where you’ll transfer to an Ice Explorer – a specialized bus that's equipped to handle the icy terrain – which will transport you out onto the ice. You'll be allowed 20 minutes to explore the glacier before heading back. Bring a water bottle to have a sip of water straight from the glacier. Day tours from Banff leave at 8 a.m. from May to early October. Departing shuttles to the Ice Explorers leave the Columbia Icefield Glacier Discovery Centre hourly. After exploring the glacier, you'll board the shuttle bus to head to the Glacier Skywalk, a glass-floored observation platform that juts out 918 feet over the Sunwapta Valley. If you don’t have time do the entire Columbia Icefield experience, you can also just book tickets for the skywalk.

Recent visitors said this is a can't-miss sight in the Rocky Mountains, calling the glacier "breathtaking" and the guides informative and entertaining. If it's an experience you know you want to do, reviewers suggested booking tickets in advance as the tours to the glacier from the Discovery Centre – operated by Brewster Sightseeing – can get crowded and you may have to wait a few hours before departing on your tour if you neglect to reserve a spot. The crowds, along with the steep ticket prices, were a common gripe among visitors.

Tours are available daily from May to mid-October; hours vary by month, but generally tours operate from 10 or 11 a.m. to 4 or 5 p.m. It's recommended that you set aside three hours for the excursion, and wear layers and appropriate shoes (waterproof shoes or hiking boots are best).

Tickets for the Columbia Icefield

Tickets for the combined Glacier Skywalk and Ice Explorer Glacier Tour cost CA$109 (about $81) for adults 16 and older; the experience costs CA$70.85 (approximately $53) for children between the ages of 6 and 15. Prices increase as you get closer to the date and surge pricing is in effect. You’ll get the best price by booking in advance online. If you decide to drive, you'll find ample parking is available at the Glacier Discovery Centre, where the Ice Explorers depart. For information, visit the Brewster Sightseeing website.
