U.S. News Insider Tip: Visitors should research and purchase parking ahead of their UT event as there is no free parking on campus. Ride-share and public transportation are also options. – Catherine Parker

In addition to being home to 52,000 college students, the University of Texas also shelters several attractions and venues that appeal to residents and visitors alike. Concert-goers head to the newly completed Moody Center for big name musical acts and UT basketball games. Meanwhile, the Bass Concert Hall hosts Broadway shows and smaller traveling acts. The open-air Darrell K. Royal-Texas Memorial Stadium vibrates on UT football game days with seating for more than 100,000 fans.

After undergoing a major renovation, the reimagined Texas Science and Natural History Museum (formerly the Texas Memorial Museum) reopened in 2023 with its "Texas Titans" – replicas of a Tyrannosaur and a Quetzalcoatlus northropi that were based off of skeletons discovered by UT researchers in Big Bend National Park. The museum's collection also features fossils and the Texas Wildlife Gallery.

Other attractions worth checking out include the Harry Ransom Center, which boasts a collection of nearly 1 million books, 5 million photographs and 42 million manuscripts, including a complete Gutenberg Bible. What's more, the LBJ Presidential Library is located on the UT campus as is the Blanton Art Museum.

Recent visitors to UT mentioned it was a lovely campus with beautiful landscaping and interesting architecture; several mentioned the iconic University of Texas Tower specifically. Many visitors were impressed by the massive Darrell K. Royal Stadium. Most enjoyed their time on campus and recommended walking around if in the area.

The University of Texas is located north of the Texas State Capitol and the Bullock Texas State History Museum and bordered by the Drag, also known as Guadalupe Street and Interstate 35. It is free to stroll; tickets are required for sporting events and performances. Most campus parking is permitted for university use, though paid public parking garages are scattered throughout the campus. For the stadium, LBJ Library, Texas Science and Natural History Museum and Bass Concert Hall, drivers should use the San Jacinto Garage. Additionally, surface lot 39 is next to the LBJ Library. The Moody Center parking is best reserved online. For more information on the University of Texas, visit its main website.
