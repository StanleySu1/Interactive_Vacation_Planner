How we rank things to do

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Statue of Liberty is at the top of many travelers’ must-see lists. A feat of 19th-century technical engineering and a beacon for immigrants arriving at Ellis Island, the statue can be admired from several different vantage points around Manhattan, including Battery Park and the High Line. But to truly appreciate its size and significance, you’ll want to visit the statue and Ellis Island while on your NYC vacation.

How to Get to the Statue of Liberty

The only way to access the Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island is by using the official National Park Service ferry operator, Statue City Cruises. If you’re departing from the New York side, you’ll hop on a ferry at Battery Park in lower Manhattan. If you’re departing from the New Jersey side, you’ll depart from Liberty State Park in Jersey City. The ferry ride takes approximately 15 minutes.

Statue of Liberty Tickets

Tickets, which cost $25 per adult and $16 per child ages 4 to 12 no matter where you depart from, cover round-trip ferry service, audio tours of Liberty and Ellis islands, as well as access to the grounds and museums on both islands. The first ferry departs from either New York or New Jersey at 9 a.m. and the last ferry departs at 4 p.m. However, according to the National Park Service, if you depart for Liberty Island any later than 2 p.m., you will not have enough time to see both Liberty and Ellis islands; you’ll have to pick just one. 

Tips for visiting

To make the most of your day, recent travelers offer a few tips. One, get in line extra early so that you can get on one of the first boats. Two, purchase a ticket and catch the ferry at Liberty State Park in Jersey City (you'll experience the same tour, but face shorter lines). Many recent visitors also recommend buying a ticket to the pedestal, which must be reserved in advance (quantities are limited). The pedestal stands at about half the height of the monument and offers panoramic views of New Jersey, New York City, the New York Harbor and Ellis Island. 

Travelers can also visit the Statue of Liberty’s crown. After climbing up 162 stairs from the pedestal, you'll be rewarded with sweeping views of the city. Tickets must be purchased online in advance via the Statue City Cruises' website. Travelers say you'll also want to book tickets several months in advance, as tickets sell out quickly.

After you’ve toured Liberty Island, hop back on the ferry boat to visit Ellis Island. On the island, swing by the Main Building and the Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration housed inside. Here, you'll find exhibits displaying some of the family heirlooms and keepsakes that immigrants brought over on their journey, as well as historic photographs. You can also do a little digging on your own family tree at the Family History Center. Other exhibits explore what immigration was like before and after Ellis Island.

Statue of Liberty tours

Though an audio guide is included with your ferry ticket and NPS rangers host free guided tours, some visitors opt to book a guided tour with a third-party operator. These small-group tours typically include a guide and round-trip ferry tickets, and are popular with travelers who want a more in-depth narrative than the audio tour can provide. 

Several of the city's best boat tours circle the Statue of Liberty, making for some spectacular photos (as does the free Staten Island Ferry). And for a bird's-eye view, consider one of the best New York City helicopter tours. The ferry that leaves from Battery Park is accessible from the Bowling Green subway stop; you can take the 4 or 5 train and visit any day except Dec. 25.

Check out the official website for more information or go to the Statue City Cruises website for boat schedules, information and to purchase tickets online.
