U.S. News Insider Tip: Start your day with a sunrise walk on the beach. Early rises get their pick of the seashells and maybe even sand dollars that have washed ashore. – Susan B. Barnes

Though still recovering from Hurricane Ian, Fort Myers Beach is open for business and remains a big draw for travelers visiting the area. About 15 miles south of the city on Estero Island, it can take as long as an hour to get there in peak traffic. Still, travelers who head to the shoreline can expect wide swaths of clean, white sand as fine as powdered sugar, which is well worth the journey for many.

Fort Myers Beach is an excellent spot for swimming and sunbathing, as well as water sports like kayaking and Jet Skiing. Rental shops for water sports equipment are positioned along the beachfront, and travelers can also reserve equipment ahead of time online through companies like Fun 'N Sun Beach & Bike Rentals. You'll find restaurants scattered along the island. The popular Times Square (a shopping and dining plaza) is still rebuilding and many of its businesses are operating out of trucks and temporary buildings.

Recent travelers share that even with the ongoing restoration in the wake of Hurricane Ian, Fort Myers Beach is still a beautiful place to visit and worth supporting during its rebuild. Others noted terrific shelling and clean beaches, though traffic can be slow moving. 

Limited public bathrooms are available, and there are spots along the beach where vacationers can rent beach chairs and umbrellas. Some hotels also lend chairs to non-hotel guests for a fee. Dogs are permitted, but they must be kept on a nonretractable 6-foot leash at all times. To learn more, visit the Fort Myers Beach website.

Hours

The beach opens at sunrise and closes 30 minutes after sunset.

Parking

Fort Myers Beach is free to visit, but there is a fee to park. Visitors can find short-term, metered parking for about $5 per hour. Metered parking spaces are monitored from 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. every day, including holidays, and some spots may have a time limit. Be sure to read all applicable signs before you leave your car. Travelers can pay for parking through the Passport Parking smartphone app.
