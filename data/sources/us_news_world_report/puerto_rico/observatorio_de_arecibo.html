How we rank things to do

The Arecibo Observatory has a rich history. Scientists used the site's massive telescope to collect data about Earth's atmosphere, the solar system's planets, comets and more since its debut in the 1960s. Though Puerto Rico may seem like an odd place for this important device, it was the perfect location. Situated near the equator, the telescope was in the ideal spot not only to study the Earth's ionosphere (its original purpose) but also to study planets. In addition, Puerto Rico could accommodate the space needed for the telescope's reflecting mirror: The dish is 1,000 feet in diameter and 167 feet deep.

In 2020, cables holding the central platform broke and the platform collapsed into the dish, damaging the dish. In 2022, the National Science Foundation announced it plans to open a STEM research facility on-site but funding would not include rebuilding the telescope.

So what can visitors do at the observatory today? They can view exhibits about atmospheric science, attend lectures and watch informative movies at the visitor center. Plus, they can visit the observation deck to see the telescope and its radio mirror. There is also a gift shop on-site.

Past travelers said the observatory is a great attraction in Puerto Rico even though the museum is small; it's a great place to take kids. Some visitors bemoan the hike to the observatory: it's a steep climb with many stairs from the parking lot to the Science & Visitor Center; however, transportation is available to the top.

The observatory is located in Arecibo, approximately 60 miles southwest of San Juan and 50 miles east of Rincón. It's also about 10 miles southwest of Cueva Ventana; combining the two would make a great day trip if you don't mind navigating some winding roads.

The Science & Visitor Center is open from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. Wednesday through Sunday. Tickets cost $15 for adults and $12 for children ages 5 to 12. Visit the observatory's website for more information.
