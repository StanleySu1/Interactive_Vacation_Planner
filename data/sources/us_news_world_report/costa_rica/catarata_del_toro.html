How we rank things to do

Measuring approximately 300 feet, this waterfall plunges over the edge of an extinct volcanic crater and is surrounded by lush tropical landscaping and mist. Unlike some of the country's other popular waterfalls, admiring Catarata del Toro doesn't necessarily require a strenuous hike. The waterfall is privately owned, and can be viewed from the on-site restaurant and several lookout points. If you'd like to get closer, there is a trail departing from the visitor center.

The first part of the self-guided trail is flat and features three lookout points. The second part of the hike requires that you traverse more than 350 concrete steps. The total length of the trail is about 2 miles round-trip. Though you can't swim in the pool at the bottom of the falls, it's still worth the trek for the views alone, according to recent vacationers.

Blue Falls

For even more views, consider joining a guided hike to Blue Falls (also known as Cataratas Las Gemelas). These falls, which are world-famous for their striking aquamarine waters, are owned by the same establishment that manages Catarata del Toro and are located within the same area.

According to recent visitors, the stairs to Catarata del Toro  are a challenge to hike and most advised taking it slow. Others visited in the rainy season and said the view of the waterfall was stunning. Recent visitors who made the trek to the Blue Falls called it the most stunning waterfall in Costa Rica. Other visitors commented on the abundance of hummingbirds at Catarata del Toro.

How to get to Catarata del Toro

Catarata del Toro is located halfway between the international airport (SJO) and La Fortuna, about 45 miles northwest of San José (the drive can take close to two hours).  

Hours

It's open Monday to Saturday from 7 a.m. to 5 p.m. (last entry at 4:30 p.m.).

Tickets

Admission to Catarata del Toro costs $14 for adults and $7 for kids and seniors ages 65 and older. Parking is free. Combo tickets are also available if you'd like to visit Catarata del Toro and the Blue Falls. Reservations are not required.

Note: Swimming is not allowed in Catarata del Toro, only the Blue Falls is open to swimming.

For information about the waterfalls and the guided tours, visit the official website.
