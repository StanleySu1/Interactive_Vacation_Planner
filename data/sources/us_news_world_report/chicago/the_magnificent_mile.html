How we rank things to do

U.S. News Insider Tip: The Magnificent Mile, known for its world-class shopping, also has plenty to offer architecture buffs. Book a walking tour with the Chicago Architecture Center and hear the untold stories of North Michigan Avenue's architectural icons. Tours start at $30. – Sarah Estime

For shopping at more than 450 retailers within walking distance in downtown Chicago, make your way to The Magnificent Mile.

Highlights

This portion of Michigan Avenue – which stretches between Lake Shore Drive and the Chicago River – beckons to shopaholics with department stores and luxury retailers like Bloomingdale's, Marcus, Macy's, Tory Burch and Nordstrom. Additionally, The Magnificent Mile is home to several top-notch eateries and luxury hotels, including The Drake, the Sofitel Chicago Magnificent Mile and the InterContinental Chicago Magnificent Mile.

Architecture buffs will also appreciate a stroll down this street thanks to its eclectic collection of buildings. While you're walking around, turn your eyes upward for views of the Chicago Water Tower, which survived the Great Chicago Fire of 1871 and is the longest standing structure on Michigan Avenue. Other structural highlights include the Wrigley Building (once the headquarters of chewing gum magnate William Wrigley Jr.) and the Tribune Tower (former home to the Chicago Tribune daily newspaper).

What recent travelers say

Reviewers compare The Mag Mile to Rodeo Drive in Los Angeles and Fifth Avenue in New York City. In addition to retail therapy, you will find attractions like Starbucks Roastery Reserve, the world's largest Starbucks.

And though you'll want to walk to get the best views of the street's architecture, if you start to tire, hop onto one of several buses – route Nos. 3, 10 (during summer), 29, 65, 66, 125, 143, 146, 147, 148, 151 and 157 – to get around the area. You can reach The Magnificent Mile on foot from the Loop via the DuSable Bridge, by bus, by L (the closest station is the Red Line's Chicago stop), by taxi or by car. For a unique perspective, consider signing up for one of the best Chicago boat tours, many of which cruise by the famous avenue when gliding down the Chicago River. Multiple parking garages are available nearby for a fee, and public restrooms are provided in the Water Tower Place shopping center.

Hours

The Magnificent Mile is free to explore at any time of day or night, though stores and other facilities adhere to their own hours.

To learn more about what there is to see and do along this famed thoroughfare, visit the Magnificent Mile's website.
