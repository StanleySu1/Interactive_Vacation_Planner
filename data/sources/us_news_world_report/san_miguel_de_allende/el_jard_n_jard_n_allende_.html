U.S. News Insider Tip: Close to El Jardín, on Calle Relox, sits a burgundy-painted house where Ignacio Allende and other independence fighters held secret meetings in the basement under the pretext of dancing; look out for the plaque on the wall. – Laura French

El Jardín is San Miguel's main plaza, located in the heart of the city. At the Zocalo, as El Jardín is often called, you can spend time people-watching, perusing the nearby shops and restaurants, and reorienting yourself should the town's winding roads lead you astray. While you're here, you might witness a local fiesta, mariachi band or live concert. Giant mojiganga puppets – hailing from the region and made from papier-mâché – are also often spotted dancing around the square, and you might just spot a wedding parade coming out of the Parroquia de San Miguel Arcángel.

Recent travelers loved wandering around the square, checking out the nearby restaurants and listening to live music. Early evenings and weekend afternoons are particularly busy times in the plaza; this is when you'll find expats and locals catching up with friends or relaxing on the wrought-iron benches.

San Miguel de Allende Tours

The square is also the starting point for many of the city's tours; among them are outings offered by local children's charity Patronato Pro Niños which tell of San Miguel's history. Tours leave on Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays at 10 a.m. and include a stop at the Parroquia de San Miguel Arcángel (on the south side of the main plaza). A tour fee of 400 pesos (about $21) is donated to the nonprofit. For more information about the tour, visit the charity's dedicated tour website. Free Tour Expert also offers daily free walking tours from outside the Parroquia at 10.30 a.m. (tips are expected, and it's best to book in advance on the website).

El Jardín is free to visit anytime. However, you may want to bring a bit of cash with you to tip musicians or purchase a snack from a local vendor.
