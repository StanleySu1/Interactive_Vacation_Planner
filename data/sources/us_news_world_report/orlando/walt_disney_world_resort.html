U.S. News Insider Tip : When park hopping, park at Hollywood Studios to limit driving from park to park. From there, you can get to Animal Kingdom by bus, EPCOT's World Showcase via the Friendship Boat or Skyliner and Magic Kingdom from the EPCOT monorail. – Johnaé De Felicis

Excessive, exhausting and expensive, Walt Disney World Resort is above all else enchanting. Composed of four theme parks, two water parks, more than two dozen hotels and the Disney Springs complex, this sprawling property features iconic sights and rides like Cinderella Castle, "it's a small world," Spaceship Earth, Toy Story Land and the Tree of Life. Even Anaheim's Disneyland can't compete, according to some.

Magic Kingdom 

Magic Kingdom – the original Disney World theme park – houses the park's most iconic rides and attractions. It's here you'll find Cinderella's castle, the spinning tea cups at Mad Tea Party, and the Seven Dwarfs Mine Train, as well as your favorite characters. It's also home to exhilarating thrill rides like Space Mountain and the new TRON Lightcycle / Run.

EPCOT 

At Disney's EPCOT– which stands for Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow – the World Showcase gets the most foot traffic. This area of the park features 11 country pavilions that showcase dishes from around the globe. You'll also find Remy's Ratatouille Adventure, a 4D ride that navigates you through Gustaeu's kitchen; the song-filled Frozen Ever After boat ride and the intense Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind that takes you on a "tour" through the galaxy.

Disney's Hollywood Studios

Hollywood Studios is filled with action (and plenty of photo-ops to boot), with two of its largest attractions being Toy Story Land and Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge. This park also has a diverse selection of live entertainment, like the beloved Indiana Jones Epic Stunt Spectacular! and Beauty and the Beast – Live on Stage. Thrill-seekers can hop on rides like the Twilight Zone Tower of Terror and Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith.

Disney's Animal Kingdom

At Animal Kingdom, you're in for the adventure of your life. There's the Kilimanjaro Safaris – an open-air safari ride that puts you just steps away from more than 30 species (i.e., giraffes, elephants, lions and more). Then you have Pandora – The World of Avatar with two rides that take you through Pandora. Creature-centered shows like the "Festival of the Lion King" and "It's Tough to be a Bug" are also on offer.

Disney Springs

Here, you'll find plenty of shopping, food, entertainment and family-friendly activities. Love Dole Whip and jumbo turkey legs? Find them at Swirls on the Water and The Smokehouse at The House of Blues, respectively. Entertainment options include the Cirque du Soleil "Drawn to Life" show, a hot air balloon ride and an AMC movie theater.

Disney's BoardWalk 

The BoardWalk features a 1/4-mile strip of unique shops, vibrant nightlife and exquisite dining. Street performers add to the Coney Island-esque vibe of this promenade. Parking and admission to Disney's BoardWalk is free as long as you park at one of Disney's resorts, and use the resort's transportation options to get to the actual BoardWalk.

Typhoon Lagoon

Typhoon Lagoon, one of Walt Disney World's two water parks, is a watery wonderland complete with a surf pool, hair-raising water rides and playgrounds where the kiddos can splash and play.

Blizzard Beach

Walt Disney World's second water park, Blizzard Beach, is a destination to chill and have fun. If you want to chill and splash, visit here. Its top water rides include the Summit Plummet, the Toboggan Racers and the Runoff Rapids.

Of course, there is one major drawback. Walt Disney World park tickets are expensive. Single-day, single park tickets start at $109 for people 10 and older. Meanwhile, one-day water park tickets start at $74. Most parkgoers suggest you visit this expansive property over the course of several days to justify the high entrance fees. If you have limited time, consider skipping the parks in favor of exploring Disney Springs or Disney's BoardWalk, which are free to visit.

Another disappointment to some: the abundance of people. During school breaks, the parks are packed with tourists and locals, leading to congested pathways and long waits. To avoid these issues, try visiting on a weekday when school is in session. Or, stay at a Walt Disney World Resort hotel, where guests have access to extended hours at select parks and attractions. Utilizing the park's Genie+ service can also help you make the most of your time in Disney World, as it's a way to trim ride wait times. Select rides have a "lightning lane" entrance with shorter wait queues and Genie+ grants you entry to a select number of lightning lane entrances per visit. What's more, select Disney resorts also include dining plans in vacation packages.

Walt Disney World Resort is located in Bay Lake and Lake Buena Vista, Florida, about 16 miles southwest of downtown Orlando. Each theme park has different hours, but all generally open by 9 a.m. and close between 6 and 11 p.m. Water park hours are from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. daily. All park visitors have access to complimentary bus, ferry or monorail transfers. Fee-based parking lots and stops for LYNX's Nos. 56 and 300 routes are also available. You'll find plenty of shops and restaurants throughout. Check out Walt Disney World Resort's website for further details.
