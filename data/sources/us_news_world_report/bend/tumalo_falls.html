How we rank things to do

Tumalo Falls almost look too perfect to be real. Picture a frothy curtain of water nearly 100 feet high free falling over a sheer cliff cut from a fragrant forest, all blended together in a thunderous display of postcard-worthy pictures. With easy access to viewpoints overlooking the falls, and a fun network of hiking and biking trails nearby, this part of the Deschutes National Forest is a very popular outing that's nearby downtown.

There are two viewpoints, a lower one and an upper one, and both are worth visiting. The trail to the top of the falls is steep but short. Hikers and mountain bikers can find trails that form the Tumalo Falls Loop, a 7-mile circuit that extends back to a fairytale meadow called Happy Valley before looping back toward the parking lot. An easier 6.5-mile trail runs along Tumalo Creek.

Visitors often mention how beautiful the falls are but some lament the limited parking. As always, arrive early and you will avoid a lot of those hassles. While you may think you need to skip the falls in the winter, don't: Hikers say the hike to the falls and the falls themselves are gorgeous blanketed in snow. (Just look out for ice.)

Location and hours

To get to the falls, drive west on Skyliner Road for about 12 miles from the edge of town. The road forks at Tumalo Creek; go right on the gravel road for about another 2.5 miles to reach the parking area. The falls themselves and the trails around them are open year round but the gravel road to the parking area will close from November to May when the snow begins to fall. You can still access the falls by on foot (cross-country skiers and snowshoers often come here), a journey that's about 5 miles round trip.

Tickets

The falls themselves are free to see, but you'll need a Northwest Forest Pass to park here ($5, daily)
